### **1. Fidelity (Accuracy of Meaning)**
**Score: 8/10**
**Justification:**
- The LLM translation captures the core meaning of the original but introduces minor deviations:
  - **Original:** "يولد الناس أحرارًا" (*"Men are born free"*) → **LLM:** "الرجال يولدون" (*"Men are born"*).
    - The original uses **"الناس"** (*"people"*), which is gender-neutral and aligns with the universal intent of the declaration. The LLM’s **"الرجال"** (*"men"*) is narrower and could be interpreted as excluding women, though modern Arabic often uses "رجال" generically.
  - **Original:** "المنفعة العامة" (*"common good"*) → **LLM:** "المصلحة العامة" (*"public interest"*).
    - Both terms are close in legal Arabic, but "المنفعة العامة" is slightly broader (encompassing collective benefit), while "المصلحة العامة" leans toward "public interest" (a more administrative term). The original’s phrasing is more philosophical.

    - The LLM’s **"لا يمكن"** (*"cannot"*) is functionally equivalent to **"لا يجوز"** (*"may not"*), but the latter is more legally precise (implying prohibition rather than impossibility).

**Overall Assessment:**
The LLM translation is largely faithful but loses some universality ("الناس" → "الرجال") and philosophical nuance ("المنفعة العامة" → "المصلحة العامة").

**Recommendations:**
- Replace **"الرجال"** with **"الناس"** to maintain gender neutrality.
- Revert to **"المنفعة العامة"** for consistency with the original’s philosophical tone.
