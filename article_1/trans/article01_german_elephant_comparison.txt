### **1. Fidelity (Accuracy to Original Meaning)**
**Score: 8/10**
**Justification:**
- The LLM translation captures the core meaning but deviates slightly in phrasing and emphasis.
  - **Original (canonical):** *"Die Menschen sind und bleiben von Geburt frei und gleich an Rechten."*
    → Emphasizes **permanence** ("bleiben") and **birthright** ("von Geburt").
  - **LLM:** *"Die Menschen sind von Natur aus frei und in ihren Rechten gleich."*
    → Replaces "von Geburt" (by birth) with "von Natur aus" (by nature), which is philosophically similar but less legally precise. "Von Natur aus" could imply a broader, more abstract concept (e.g., natural law) rather than the specific legal claim of birthright.
  - **Original:** *"Gesellschaftliche Unterschiede können nur nach gemeinschaftlichem Nutzen begründet sein."*
    → "Nach gemeinschaftlichem Nutzen" = "based on communal benefit" (focus on the *process* of justification).
  - **LLM:** *"Soziale Unterschiede können nur zum gemeinen Wohl gegründet werden."*
    → "Zum gemeinen Wohl" = "for the common good" (focus on the *outcome*). While synonymous, the original’s phrasing is more procedural (how distinctions are *justified*), whereas the LLM’s is more teleological (what they *serve*).

**Overall Assessment:**
The LLM translation is largely faithful but sacrifices some legal precision for naturalness. The shift from "von Geburt" to "von Natur aus" and from "nach gemeinschaftlichem Nutzen" to "zum gemeinen Wohl" are subtle but meaningful in a legal context.

**Recommendation:**
- Restore "von Geburt" to match the original’s emphasis on birthright.
- Consider reverting to "nach gemeinschaftlichem Nutzen" for procedural clarity, though "zum gemeinen Wohl" is not incorrect.
