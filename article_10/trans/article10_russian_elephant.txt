Here’s a detailed evaluation of the **two Chinese translations** of the original text (inspired by **Article 10 of the French Declaration of the Rights of the Citizen**):

---

### **Original Text (English)**
*"No one may be disturbed on account of his opinions, even religious ones, as long as the manifestation of such opinions does not interfere with the established Law and Order."*

---

### **Canonical Chinese Translation (官方译本)**
*"意见的发表只要不扰乱法律所规定的公共秩序，任何人都不得因其意见、甚至信教的意见而遭受干涉。"*

### **LLM Chinese Translation (大模型译本)**
*"只要意见的表达不妨碍既定的法律和秩序，任何人都不得因其观点（包括宗教观点）而受到打扰。"*

## **Evaluation Criteria**

### **1. Fidelity (0-10)**
**Score: 8.5/10**
**Justification:**
- **Core meaning preserved**:
  - *"任何人都不得因其意见而遭受干涉"* (canonical) → *"任何人都不得因其观点而受到打扰"* (LLM)
    - Both mean "no one may be disturbed for their opinions," but:
      - *"遭受干涉"* is **stronger** ("suffer interference") and more **legal/formal**.
      - *"受到打扰"* is **softer** ("be disturbed/bothered"), which **dilutes the legal force slightly**.
  - *"甚至信教的意见"* → *"包括宗教观点"*
    - **Equivalent**: Both mean "including religious opinions."
    - *"甚至"* (canonical) is **more emphatic**, while *"包括"* (LLM) is **more neutral/modern**.
  - *"意见的发表"* → *"意见的表达"*
    - **Near-equivalent**: Both mean "manifestation/expression of opinions."
    - *"发表"* is **slightly narrower** (implies public declaration), while *"表达"* is **broader** (covers any form of expression).
  - *"不扰乱法律所规定的公共秩序"* → *"不妨碍既定的法律和秩序"*
    - **Key deviation**:
      - *"法律所规定的公共秩序"* = "public order **as defined by law**" (emphasizes **legal basis**).
      - *"既定的法律和秩序"* = "established **law and order**" (broader, but **less precise**—could imply "law" and "order" as separate concepts).
    - The LLM version **loses the explicit link to "public order"** and **weakens the legal specificity**.

**Overall Assessment:**
- **High fidelity**, but **two notable deviations**:
  1. *"遭受干涉"* → *"受到打扰"* (weakens legal force).
