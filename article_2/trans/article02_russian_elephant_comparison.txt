### **1. Fidelity (Accuracy of Meaning)**
**Score: 7/10**
**Justification:**
- **"Сохранение" (preservation) vs. "обеспечение" (ensuring/protection):**
  The original uses *"обеспечение"* (ensuring, safeguarding), which implies active protection, while *"сохранение"* (preservation) suggests passive maintenance. The latter weakens the proactive legal-philosophical intent.
- **"Непреложных" (inviolable) vs. "неотъемлемых" (inalienable):**
  Both are correct, but *"неотъемлемых"* (inalienable) is the canonical term in Russian legal texts, aligning with the original’s emphasis on rights that cannot be revoked. *"Непреложных"* is archaic and less precise.
- **"Сопротивление оскорблению" (resistance to insult) vs. "сопротивление угнетению" (resistance to oppression):**
  *"Оскорбление"* (insult) is a **critical mistranslation**. The original refers to *oppression* (systemic injustice), not personal insult. This alters the legal meaning entirely.
- **"Право на сопротивление" (right to resistance) vs. "сопротивление" (resistance):**
  The LLM adds *"право на"* (right to), which is redundant—*"сопротивление"* alone suffices as a right in the original.

**Overall:** The LLM captures the general idea but introduces inaccuracies that distort the legal-philosophical precision of the original.
