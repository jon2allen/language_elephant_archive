## **2. Cross-Language Analysis**

### **2.1 Common Patterns of Error**
1. **Legal Terminology Precision**
   - **Issue:** Some languages (Chinese, Arabic, Japanese) used less precise legal terms (e.g., "unshakable" instead of "inalienable").
   - **Impact:** Reduces fidelity to the original legal intent.

2. **Over-Literal Translations**
   - **Issue:** Japanese and Chinese translations were **too literal**, leading to awkward phrasing.
   - **Impact:** Sacrifices natural readability for word-for-word accuracy.

3. **Semantic Drift in Key Concepts**
   - **Issue:** "Oppression" was sometimes translated as "injustice" (Arabic) or "tyranny" (Japanese).
   - **Impact:** Alters the original philosophical meaning.

4. **Grammatical/Syntactic Issues**
   - **Issue:** Japanese and Arabic had **unnatural word order** in places.
   - **Impact:** Reduces fluency and readability.
