### **1. Fidelity (Accuracy of Meaning)**
**Score: 8/10**
**Justification:**
- The LLM translation captures the core meaning: sovereignty resides in the Nation, and no authority can be exercised without its origin in the Nation.
- **Key divergence**: The original uses *"n'en émane expressément"* (literally "expressly emanates from it"), while the LLM uses *"ne procède pas directement de la Nation"* ("does not proceed directly from the Nation").
  - *"Émane expressément"* implies a **formal, explicit delegation** of authority (a legal nuance), whereas *"procède directement"* suggests a **direct, unmediated origin** (a more philosophical or abstract link).
  - The original’s *"nul corps, nul individu"* (no body, no individual) is slightly more emphatic than *"aucun corps ni aucun individu"* (no body nor any individual), but the difference is minor.

**Overall Assessment:**
The LLM translation is **largely faithful** but weakens the **legal precision** of the original by replacing *"émane expressément"* with *"procède directement."*

**Recommendation:**
Replace *"ne procède pas directement"* with *"n'émane pas expressément"* to restore the original’s legal nuance.
