### **1. Fidelity (0–10)**
**Score: 8/10**
**Justification:**
- **Accurate core meaning**: The LLM translation correctly conveys the essence of the original, including the concepts of the "general will," citizen participation, equality before the law, and merit-based access to public offices.
- **Minor deviations**:
  - *"an seiner Bildung teilzuhaben"* (lit. "to participate in its formation") is slightly less precise than the canonical *"an seiner Formung mitzuwirken"* ("to co-shape its formation"). "Bildung" can imply "education" or "creation," which is narrower than "Formung" (shaping/formation).
  - *"für alle Würden und öffentlichen Ämter geeignet"* ("eligible for all dignities and public offices") omits the nuance of *"zugänglich"* ("accessible") in the original, which emphasizes openness rather than mere suitability.
  - *"nach ihren Fähigkeiten ohne Unterschied, außer nach Tugenden und Talenten"* is grammatically correct but less elegant than the original’s *"nachdem sie durch ihre Fähigkeiten... dazu fähig sind"* ("after they are capable through their abilities..."). The LLM version simplifies the conditional structure.

**Overall**: The LLM translation is largely faithful but loses some precision in legal phrasing and nuance.

**Overall**: The LLM translation is highly natural and grammatically sound, though it occasionally sacrifices stylistic elegance for clarity.

**Score: 8.5/10**
**Justification:**

**Overall**: The LLM captures the intent well but misses some rhetorical and philosophical depth.

   - *"Alle Bürger"* instead of *"Jeder Bürger"* for consistency with the original’s spirit.
