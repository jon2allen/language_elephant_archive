Here’s a detailed evaluation of the LLM translation compared to the original (canonical) version of **Article 7** of the French *Déclaration des Droits de l'Homme et du Citoyen (1789)*:

### **1. Fidelity (0-10)**
**Score: 8/10**
**Justification:**
- The LLM translation captures the core meaning of the original but introduces minor deviations:
  - **"Nul homme" → "Aucune personne"**: The original uses the gendered "homme" (man/human), while the LLM opts for the gender-neutral "personne." This is a modern adaptation but slightly alters the historical tone.
  - **"ni détenu" → "ou emprisonnée"**: "Détenu" (detained) is broader than "emprisonnée" (imprisoned), which is more restrictive. The original includes all forms of detention, not just imprisonment.
  - **"dans les cas déterminés par la loi" → "sauf dans les cas... prescrits par la loi"**: The LLM’s phrasing is accurate but less concise. The original’s "déterminés" (determined) is stronger than "prescrits" (prescribed).
  - **"exécutent ou font exécuter" → "exécute ou cause l’exécution"**: The LLM simplifies the structure but retains the meaning.
  - **"il se rend coupable par la résistance" → "car la résistance constitue une infraction"**: The LLM’s version is clearer but loses the original’s active phrasing ("il se rend coupable" = "he makes himself guilty").

**Overall:** The LLM translation is largely faithful but modernizes and slightly narrows the scope in places.

### **3. Intent Preservation (0-10)**
**Score: 7/10**
**Justification:**
- The **legal-philosophical intent** is preserved in broad strokes, but nuances are weakened:
  - **Original intent**: The text establishes **due process** (arrest/detention only by law), **accountability for arbitrary orders**, and **unconditional obedience to lawful authority**. The LLM captures this but with less force.
  - **"Nul homme" vs. "Aucune personne"**: The original’s "homme" reflects Enlightenment universalism (rights for all humans), while "personne" is more neutral but less evocative.
  - **"il se rend coupable par la résistance"**: The original’s active phrasing ("he makes himself guilty") emphasizes personal responsibility, whereas the LLM’s passive "constitue une infraction" (is an offense) is more bureaucratic.
  - **"sans délai" vs. "à l'instant"**: The original’s "à l'instant" (immediately) conveys urgency, while "sans délai" (without delay) is slightly softer.

**Overall:** The LLM’s version is legally sound but less philosophically charged.

**Suggested Revised Translation**:
*"Nul homme ne peut être accusé, arrêté ni détenu que dans les cas déterminés par la loi, et selon les formes qu’elle a prescrites.
