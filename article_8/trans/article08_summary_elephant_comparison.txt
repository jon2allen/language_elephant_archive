## **6. Recommendations for Improving Translation Quality**

### **🔹 For Elephant (Model Improvements)**
1. **Enhance Legal Terminology Training**
   - Incorporate **legal corpora** (e.g., UN documents, national constitutions, penal codes) to improve **term-of-art accuracy**.
   - Use **domain-specific fine-tuning** for **legal translations**.

2. **Improve Formality Calibration**
   - Train on **formal vs. informal registers** to ensure translations match the **tone of official documents**.
   - Use **style transfer techniques** to adjust formality levels.

3. **Address Language-Specific Weaknesses**
   - **Chinese & Japanese:** Improve **conciseness** and **legal phrasing**.
   - **Arabic:** Strengthen **legal syntax** and **term consistency**.
   - **Russian & Portuguese:** Refine **legal nuance** (e.g., "evidentemente" vs. "claramente").

4. **Post-Editing for Legal Translations**
   - Implement **human-in-the-loop review** for **high-stakes legal documents**.
   - Use **rule-based checks** for **key legal phrases** (e.g., "penalty" vs. "punishment").

5. **Benchmark Against Canonical Translations**
   - Use **UDHR, EU treaties, and national constitutions** as **gold standards** for evaluation.
   - Fine-tune on **parallel legal texts** to improve consistency.

### **🔹 For Users (Best Practices)**
1. **Always Review for Legal Use**
   - **Do not rely solely on Elephant** for **contracts, laws, or official documents** without **human review**.
2. **Use for General Understanding First**
   - **Excellent for drafts, educational materials, or informal translations**.
3. **Compare Against Canonical Versions**
   - Cross-check with **official translations** (e.g., UN, EU, national government sources).
4. **Adjust for Formality as Needed**
   - Manually refine **legal terms** and **formal phrasing** where necessary.
